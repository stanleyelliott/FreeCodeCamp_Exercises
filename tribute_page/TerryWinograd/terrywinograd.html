Terry Winograd
Terry Winograd is a researcher and computer scientist known for his work on artificial intelligence and natural parsing; His dissertation project SHRDLU was one of the earliest parsers for natural language. He was also the advisor for Google founder Larry Page at Stanford.

SHRDLU was a program that simulated a very simple virtual world containing 3d shapes. It also understood a limited subset of the english language and was capable of parsing complex instructions such as "Move the block on top of the cube to the top of the green shape".
